About Me

I am a Professor of Computer Science at Prestigious University, where I lead the Human-Centered AI Research Group. My research focuses on developing AI systems that are understandable, controllable, and beneficial to human users.

I received my Ph.D. from MIT in 2010, and have since published over 50 peer-reviewed papers in top-tier conferences and journals. My work has been recognized with several awards, including the ACM SIGCHI Best Paper Award and the NSF CAREER Award.

When I'm not researching or teaching, I enjoy hiking, photography, and playing the violin.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe in creating an inclusive learning environment where students from diverse backgrounds can thrive. My teaching approach emphasizes:

  • Active learning through hands-on projects and real-world applications
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving skills
  • Collaboration and peer learning
  • Connecting course material to current research and industry trends
